After Division of Labor

A long-form Chinese essay on how AI changes the economic logic of specialization, coordination, and organizational form.

TL;DR

Claim: modern economics is not suddenly wrong. It was built for a production world where fine-grained division of labor generated more benefit than coordination friction. AI changes that cost structure by expanding the capability radius of a single action unit.

The essay argues that as execution becomes cheaper and more general, the hidden costs of handoff, alignment, supervision, verification, and responsibility splitting become more visible. In some knowledge-work domains, the bottleneck shifts from insufficient capability to excessive coordination friction.

The Chinese title is 《分工之后》, with the working subtitle AI时代经济学上的一朵乌云.

The central diagram

The book does not say specialization disappears. It says fine-grained organizational splitting has to justify itself again when a smaller human-agent unit can preserve more context and complete a longer task loop.
